## Cadre overview

Cadre AI is an AI strategy and implementation consultancy. It helps
organizations identify high-ROI opportunities, prepare teams for adoption, and
implement workflows, automations, and AI agents intended to improve revenue,
profitability, and employee impact.

Sources: [home](https://www.cadreai.com),
[about](https://www.cadreai.com/about)

## Delivery approach

Cadre describes a four-part strategy process:

1. Discover valuable use cases.
2. Survey available tools and emerging capabilities.
3. Implement selected solutions and train teams.
4. Scale successful work and establish a longer-term roadmap.

Cadre says it activates existing tools when they fit, automates across current
systems where appropriate, and builds custom agents when simpler options are
insufficient.

## AI Maturity Index

Cadre says its AI Maturity Index assesses an organization across an eight-pillar
transformation framework and produces grades, explanations, and improvement
guidance. The published pillars are:

1. Dedicated AI team
2. AI command center
3. AI-first culture shift
4. AI-enabled technology stack
5. AI-healthy data
6. AI agent readiness
7. Departmental AI deep dives
8. Long-term AI vision

Within the AI Transformation Intensive, the index helps establish the current
state and informs workshops, use cases, vision, and roadmap.

Sources: [strategy](https://www.cadreai.com/strategy),
[AI Transformation Intensive](https://www.cadreai.com/ai-transformation-intensive)

## LLM selection and data security

Cadre's public guidance says model and tool selection should account for:

- Use case and reasoning complexity
- Required quality and response speed
- Cost and expected volume
- Existing technology stack
- Business goals and risk

Its guidance favors inexpensive models for high-volume straightforward tasks,
balanced models for most knowledge work, and the most capable models for complex
or high-consequence reasoning.

Cadre's public security approach emphasizes company-wide approved AI tools,
reduced use of personal accounts for business data, clear policies and
ownership, and configurations intended to keep company data from training public
models. Specific architecture, certifications, compliance requirements, and
contract terms require a strategist.

Sources: [strategy](https://www.cadreai.com/strategy),
[engineering](https://www.cadreai.com/ai-engineering),
[model-selection article](https://www.cadreai.com/articles/ai-model-selection)

## Unknown-answer behavior

When the answer is absent or client-specific:

1. Say what part cannot be verified.
2. Do not fill the gap with a plausible-sounding answer.
3. Offer the relevant public source or Cadre contact route.
4. Collect contact information only if the user requests follow-up and consents.
